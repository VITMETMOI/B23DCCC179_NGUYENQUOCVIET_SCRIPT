Facebook Global Outage

(Dân trí) - At around 10:20 PM on March 5th, many Facebook users in Vietnam suddenly couldn't log into the social network.

"I was messaging a friend on Facebook when my account suddenly logged out. I tried logging back in several times but couldn't," shared Ngọc Anh, a resident of Bình Thạnh District, HCMC.

At around 10:20 PM on March 5th, many Facebook users in Vietnam were unable to access the social network. The issue was not limited to Vietnam but affected users globally.

According to Downdetector, a specialized site for monitoring social media health, over 100,000 users worldwide reported issues with Facebook. Users reported that the outage affected both Facebook and Messenger platforms on both web and mobile.

According to our observations, the outage also affected other Meta platforms such as Messenger, Instagram, and Threads. Some Instagram users reported that their feeds could not refresh despite an active internet connection.

On the social media platform X, the hashtags "#instagramdown" and "#facebookdown" quickly trended, garnering significant attention. Meta's stock dropped by more than 1%.

"We are aware that some people are having trouble accessing our services. We are working to get things back to normal as quickly as possible," said Andy Stone, a Meta spokesperson, in a post on X.

Additionally, many users reported issues with TikTok and YouTube, unable to view videos on both platforms. Downdetector noted that most users experienced server connection errors on both web and app platforms.

Dân trí reporters are in contact with Facebook representatives in Vietnam to discuss the incident.

By around 11:20 PM, Facebook services were restored. Facebook has not yet provided any official response regarding the cause of the outage.
